# Research Gate — Go / No-Go Assessment

**Phase:** 3.5 — Research Gate (Decision Checkpoint)
**Project:** career-discovery-platform (post-pivot: Study-Abroad Counselor Copilot)
**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Verdict:** **YELLOW — Conditional Green** (5 specific conditions, listed below)

---

## What the research found

### Market
- **Real but small.** TAM ₹150–300 cr ($18–36M). SAM (G+A focused mid-size, deduplicated AAERI+AIRC pool) ₹5–7 cr.
- **Strictly-qualified primary segment: 15–25 agencies** — Year-1 SOM of 25 paid customers requires majority capture of the narrowest segment.
- **India-only ceiling: ~₹1.5 Cr ARR sustainable.** Fundable seed-level. Not Series A. Geo-expansion to Nepal at Month 13–15 is structurally required.
- Underlying Indian outbound declined 5.7% in 2025 (first contraction in 3 years), but Germany surged +15.1% YoY and Australia complexity (EL3 visa tier) increases per-student counselor workload — both tailwinds for *this specific tool*.

### Competition
- **Whitespace verified** — no incumbent productizes a counselor-side discovery PDF for Indian mid-size agencies in G+A.
- **Window: 12–18 months max.** Meritto's Mio AI suite is **6–12 months from a discovery-PDF bolt-on**. SmartX 12–18 months behind. Cialfo (via BridgeU) 18–24 months.
- **ChatGPT-as-incumbent is manageable, not fatal** — 20–45% citation error rates, no Indian context, no DPDP, no family-trust visual, no workflow integration.
- **Strategic move:** position as the discovery layer that *integrates with* Meritto/SmartX, not against them.

### Customer & demand
- **Critical reframe:** The 2–3hr discovery-session pain is **founder-reported, NOT corroborated by independent customer voice**. The real corroborated pain is post-payment trust collapse. **The PDF is a trust-restoration artifact, not a time-saver.**
- Indian SMB tailwind: 86% plan to increase software spend in 2025; SmartX has scaled to 1,000+ institutions at ₹4,999–24,999/mo (the WTP anchor).
- Counselor-side ChatGPT adoption for discovery work: **<20% best guess, no primary data** — biggest unknown.
- Sales cycle 6–12 weeks. Demo tolerance 20–30 min. Cold email dead. LinkedIn outbound = spine.

### Pricing
- **Tiers locked:** ₹2,999 / ₹7,999 / ₹14,999 per month — derived from SmartX market-clearing anchor and ChatGPT Team floor.
- Year-1 ARR target: ~₹22.5L (25 agencies × ₹7,500 blended × 12).

### Distribution & geography
- **Beachhead: Delhi NCR + Chandigarh + Punjab corridor** (~45–55 of the 120–170 deduplicated mid-size agencies in one 5-hour-drivable zone).
- AAERI cold-outreach list of ~92–117 named agencies (count needs founder reconciliation — directory data has internal inconsistency).
- Channel spine: LinkedIn outbound (founder-led), AAERI Convention August attendance (delegate, not sponsor), DPDP-for-agencies whitepaper as content moat.
- 90-day funnel: 520 LinkedIn requests → ~10 demos → 3–5 pilots → **2–4 paid by day 90.** Year-1 target of 25 closes in months 4–9.

### Regulatory
- DPDP compliance deadline **May 13, 2027** — forced-buy lever for tooling decisions.
- No mandatory MEA registration for education agents (Punjab is the one state-level exception, statute text pending primary verification).
- Cross-border data transfer at launch = non-issue (India→India).
- **Year-1 compliance cost: ₹4–8 lakh + 6–10 dev-weeks.** Material but tractable.

---

## Verdict: Yellow — Conditional Green

This is **not** a "stop here" finding. The wedge is real, the pricing is defensible, the GTM is executable for a 2-SDE side-project team, and the regulatory tailwind (DPDP May 2027) creates a forced sales event. But the green light requires **five specific conditions to become true** within the next 60–90 days, or the assessment regresses to yellow:

### Conditions for the green light

1. **Recruit 1 design-partner agency with weekly time committed, within 30 days.** The single highest-leverage action. Without this, every downstream decision is guesswork dressed as confidence.
2. **Validate the trust-restoration framing in 5+ counselor conversations.** Specifically: confirm that families value a branded take-home PDF as a payment-justification artifact, and that counselors see the PDF as a *retention* tool rather than a *disintermediation* threat.
3. **Validate or refute the 2–3hr discovery-session claim with primary data.** If it validates at 2hr+, full pricing tier math holds and time-saving becomes a *secondary* (but real) benefit. If it lands at 60–90 min, the Year-1 ARR math still works on the trust-artifact basis alone but the Large Agency ₹14,999 tier needs justification on volume not TCO.
4. **Confirm counselor-side ChatGPT adoption rate** via a 30-counselor phone survey. If adoption is >50% for discovery work, the "AI-tool replaces ChatGPT" pitch weakens and the product must differentiate on data freshness + branded delivery + DPDP. If <20% (best guess), the wedge is wider than current evidence suggests.
5. **Reconcile the AAERI directory count.** ~4–6 hours of manual website review to fix the 92-vs-117 arithmetic gap and produce the actual first-30 outreach list with strict G+A-focus criteria applied.

### What turns it red
- 0/5 design partners recruited after 60 days of focused founder-led outreach
- Counselor conversations reveal the PDF would be perceived as a disintermediation threat by buyers (i.e., the counselor's-package section does not adequately reframe)
- Meritto announces a Mio AI discovery-PDF feature in the next 3 months
- Counselor-side ChatGPT adoption survey shows >70% adoption for discovery work specifically

### What turns it solid green
- 2+ design partners signed in 30 days with weekly-time commitments
- Counselor conversations confirm trust-restoration framing AND validate 2–3hr (or anywhere ≥75 min) discovery-session time
- DPDP-for-agencies whitepaper co-authored with one named CA/lawyer
- First counselor signal that they'd pay ₹7,999/mo at end of 30-day pilot

---

## Strongest signal in favor of proceeding

The combination of (a) **forced-buy regulatory window** (DPDP May 2027), (b) **verified whitespace** in counselor-side tooling for Indian agencies, (c) **anchor-pricing reveal** from SmartX (1,000+ institutions paying ₹4,999–24,999/mo proves the WTP corridor), and (d) **AAERI + AIRC providing a ~120-name cold list** that maps directly to the buyer segment, creates a 12–18 month execution window where a 2-SDE side-project team can plausibly close 25 paying agencies for ~₹22.5L exit-Year-1 ARR. This is a meaningful business at the founder's stated capacity. It is not a venture-scale business — but it does not need to be.

## Strongest signal against proceeding

The product hypothesis rests on a **founder-asserted pain (2–3hr discovery session) that independent customer voice does not corroborate**. The reframe to trust-restoration is defensible, but the founder has not personally tested it in conversation with the buyer. Recruiting one design partner in the first 30 days is therefore not optional — it is the binary go/no-go signal for whether the entire premise survives contact with paying buyers.

## Adjacent pivot options if the conditions fail

If 60 days of founder-led outreach produces zero design partners, three adjacent pivots surface from the research:

1. **Move upstream to universities** — Cialfo / Univalley / Manifest territory. Bigger contracts, longer sales cycles, requires capital and team scale this founder does not have. **Probably not viable for this team.**
2. **Pivot to a B2C "trust-restoration report" for families paying counselors** — sell directly to parents at ₹500–2,000/PDF. Bypasses the counselor entirely. Counterintuitive but defensible if the corroborated pain is "we paid ₹X and got nothing real." **Plausible but changes the team-required GTM significantly.**
3. **Narrow further to Germany-only counselor tool** — Australia is in destination retreat; Germany is the breakout. A Germany-focused counselor tool has a much smaller market but a much sharper positioning and could co-market with DAAD India + Goethe-Institut. **Worth holding as a fallback.**
