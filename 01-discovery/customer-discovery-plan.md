# 30-Day Customer Discovery Plan

**Phase:** 3.5 → Customer Discovery (pre-Phase 4)
**Project:** Study-Abroad Counselor Copilot
**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Mission:** Convert the 5 Research Gate conditions from "needs validation" to "validated or refuted." At day 30, you should know whether to proceed to Phase 4 (Strategy) or pivot.

---

## What you are trying to learn

You are NOT validating "is my idea good." You are validating four specific, binary-answerable questions:

1. **Does the trust-restoration framing resonate?** Specifically: do counselors agree that families experience a post-payment trust gap, AND would a branded PDF the agency hands over partially address it?
2. **Is the discovery-session time roughly 2–3 hours, 60–90 minutes, or something else?** Direct measurement, not founder anecdote.
3. **What share of counselors currently use ChatGPT/Gemini for the actual discovery work** (not just admin)?
4. **Will at least one mid-size agency owner commit weekly time as a design partner**, in exchange for free access?
5. **What does the AAERI directory actually contain** after you walk it manually?

Everything else is noise.

## The counselor interview script (45 min, semi-structured)

**Goal:** Capture the counselor's actual workflow, surface pains organically, test the PDF concept WITHOUT leading the witness. Do NOT pitch the product in the first 30 minutes.

### Setup (2 min)

> "Thanks for the time. I'm at the research stage of building a tool for Indian study-abroad agencies — not selling anything today. I'd like to understand how your agency actually runs the early-stage student-and-parent discovery work, in your own words. I'll mostly listen and take notes. Is it OK if I record audio for my own reference? I won't share it."

### Section 1: Their agency (5 min)

- "Tell me about your agency. How many counselors? How many students per year?"
- "What's your destination mix? Germany, Australia, US, UK?"
- "What's the typical service package — discovery, application, visa, post-arrival?"
- "What do families pay you on average for a full engagement?"

### Section 2: The discovery session — open-ended (15 min)

> Goal: Let them describe the work. Avoid leading questions about time.

- "Walk me through what happens when a brand-new family contacts you. From the first call to the moment they've decided on a country and college shortlist."
- (If they describe a discovery meeting): "Tell me more about that meeting. Who attends? How long does it usually run? What do you cover?"
- "Who at your agency actually does the discovery conversation — you, head counselor, junior counselor?"
- "Walk me through what happens BETWEEN sessions — do you go research things, send things back, etc.?"
- "When you wrap up the discovery process, what does the family leave with? Anything physical or digital? Or is it all in the conversation?"

> **Capture these numbers verbatim:** total time spent per family in the discovery phase (including prep, conversation, follow-up); who does the work; what the family receives at the end.

### Section 3: What goes wrong (10 min)

> Goal: Surface the post-payment trust gap and other pains WITHOUT prompting.

- "What's the part of this process that gives you the most trouble?"
- "When families have a bad experience with your agency — when it goes sideways — what's usually the cause?"
- "Do families ever come back later and say 'I wish you'd told us X about this country / college / visa earlier'?"
- "Do you ever lose families to a competing agency? What's the usual reason?"
- "How do you handle the situation where the family has already researched everything on ChatGPT before they sit down with you?"

### Section 4: Tools and current workflow (8 min)

- "What tools do you currently use to do the research + shortlisting work — CRM, Excel, ChatGPT, anything else?"
- "Have you ever tried using ChatGPT or Gemini to help with the discovery process specifically?" (Probe: which job — admin, drafting, actual recommendations?)
- "What's missing from the tools you have?"

### Section 5: The PDF concept — soft probe (5 min)

> Goal: Test the trust-restoration framing without selling.

- "If I described a tool that takes the student's profile and produces a take-home PDF for the family — country recommendation, college options, costs, visa, the works — branded as your agency, in their hands at the end of the discovery session… does that solve a real problem for your agency, or does it not move the needle?"
- (If positive): "What would have to be true for you to actually use it? What would make you NOT use it?"
- (If negative): "Why not? What would be a better solve?"
- "Some agency owners worry that handing the family a polished take-home report means the family then shops competitor counselors with it. Do you share that concern, or is the opposite true (the report makes the family stickier to your agency)?"

### Section 6: Design partner ask (5 min — only in interviews 6+)

> Only if the previous sections went well.

- "I'm planning to give 3 mid-size agencies early access for a 30-day pilot, free, in exchange for weekly time + honest feedback. After the 30 days you decide whether to continue at our standard pricing or walk away — no obligation. Would you want to be one of the three?"
- (If yes): "Great. I'll send a one-page agreement this week. Practically: are you willing to commit to a 30-min weekly call for 4 weeks, plus letting me observe one real discovery session?"

### Wrap (2 min)

- "Last question — who else in your network should I talk to? Specifically someone who'd give me equally honest answers."
- "Thank you. I'll send notes from this conversation in a few days so you can correct anything I got wrong."

## The 5-question ChatGPT adoption survey

Send as a Google Form. Aim for 30 responses across the AAERI/AIRC list.

1. **What's your role at your agency?** [Owner / Head counselor / Senior counselor / Junior counselor / Other]
2. **What's your agency size?** [Solo (1) / Small (2–4) / Mid (5–20) / Large (21–50) / Enterprise (50+)]
3. **In the past 30 days, how often did you or your team use ChatGPT, Gemini, or similar AI for student-facing discovery/research work** (country recommendation, college shortlisting, cost analysis)? [Never / 1–2 times / Weekly / Daily]
4. **Same question but for admin/operational work** (drafting emails, writing SOPs, processing notes)? [Never / 1–2 times / Weekly / Daily]
5. **If you do use AI for discovery work, what's the biggest problem with the output?** [Free text]

## The one-page design-partner agreement template

> **Design Partner Agreement — Study-Abroad Counselor Copilot Pilot**
>
> **Pilot duration:** 30 days from [start date] to [end date].
>
> **What the agency gets:**
> - Full access to the tool, no charge, for the pilot duration.
> - Direct access to founders via WhatsApp / email for any issue.
> - The right to influence the product roadmap during the pilot.
> - 50% discount on first year of paid subscription IF they choose to continue at end of pilot. No obligation.
>
> **What the agency gives:**
> - A 30-min call per week for 4 weeks.
> - Permission for founders to observe one real student-family discovery session during the pilot.
> - Honest feedback — what worked, what didn't, what would have to change.
> - At end of pilot: a yes/no decision on whether to continue at the standard pricing tier.
>
> **What's NOT in this agreement:**
> - No exclusive licensing or rights.
> - No equity, revenue share, or financial obligations beyond the optional Year-1 50% discount.
> - No legal liability between parties beyond standard data handling under DPDP.
>
> **Signed:** [Founder] / [Agency owner] / [Date]

## "What good looks like" at day 30

| Condition | Validation target | Refutation signal |
|---|---|---|
| #1 Design partner | At least 1 written agreement signed | 0 signed after 60 days of focused effort |
| #2 Trust-restoration framing | ≥ 4 of 10 counselors describe a post-payment trust gap unprompted in interviews | 0 of 10 mention it; counselors uniformly dismiss the PDF as redundant |
| #3 Discovery time | Median ≥ 90 min across 10+ counselors | Median <60 min; product survives only on trust artifact, pricing math weakens at large tier |
| #4 ChatGPT adoption | <40% use it for discovery work (weekly+); >70% would still adopt a specialist tool | >70% use ChatGPT for discovery work AND say it's good enough |
| #5 AAERI directory | Reconciled count of ~92–117 with regional breakdown; first-30 list extracted | (Can't fail — this is just data hygiene) |

### Decision matrix at day 30

- **All 5 met → Phase 4 (Strategy) with strong validated framing.**
- **3–4 met with no killer signal → Phase 4 with explicit caveats; flag what's still unvalidated as Phase 8 risk.**
- **2 met, including #1 (design partner) → Phase 4 starts, but Phase 6 (Product) scope is conservative — build the minimum the design partner needs.**
- **0–1 met, especially if #1 fails → Pivot conversation. Reopen the brainstorm with: (a) Germany-only narrowed wedge, (b) B2C parent-facing PDF, (c) different category entirely.**
