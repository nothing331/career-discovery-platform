## Recommended Beachhead Metro

- **Pick:** Delhi NCR + Chandigarh corridor, treated as ONE GTM zone (drivable, 250 km, same WhatsApp circles, same AAERI sub-network).
- **Density of qualifying agencies in this zone:** 28 (Delhi NCR AAERI) + 17 (Chandigarh AAERI) + ~4–6 AIRC India-HQ overlap = **45–50 named mid-size agencies**, i.e. ~50% of the deduplicated TAM in a single 5-hour driving radius. Punjab corridor (Jalandhar, Ludhiana, Amritsar, Mohali) adds another tail of smaller G+A-only shops not in AAERI but visible in directories (Skybird Mohali, Jupiter Chandigarh-ICEF, Abroad Gateway Sector-42, APSA Delhi+Chandigarh).
- **Why this metro:**
  - **AAERI density** — single highest concentration (28 + 17 = 45) of the only association whose membership = Australia-licensed agents. Australia is half the beachhead.
  - **Australia focus** — the Punjab/Haryana/Chandigarh corridor was already flagged in Wave 1–3 as "gold for Australia-focused agencies"; Wave 4 directory scan confirms (Skybird, Jupiter, Abroad Gateway, APSA all foreground AU).
  - **Founder accessibility** — Delhi-based founder (inferred from typical Salescode-network founders + the explicit "in-person preferred, esp. Delhi NCR, Chandigarh" note in Waves 1–3) can do 3–4 in-person meetings/day in NCR and bus/train to Chandigarh weekly. Mumbai or Bangalore would require flights, dropping meeting density 3–4x.
  - **Sales-cycle friction — LOWEST** — AAERI-Delhi is a tight WhatsApp circle; one warm intro from an AAERI board member or an Australia-state-government rep typically opens 5–8 doors. Same is not true in Mumbai or Bangalore where networks are more fragmented.
  - **Germany overlap** — Delhi has DAAD office, Goethe-Institut Max Mueller Bhavan, and the German Embassy visa section; Germany-focused agencies (APSA, Edwise Delhi, Global Reach) cluster here too. The Germany side of G+A is also Delhi-heaviest.
- **What winning here proves:**
  - 8–12 paying customers in this corridor within Months 1–4 → product-market fit signal for G+A agencies.
  - Validation that ₹50k–₹2L ACV holds in the highest-WTP geography. If WTP doesn't hold here, it won't hold in Mumbai/Bangalore either.
  - Reference logos (esp. any AAERI board-member agency) become the wedge for Mumbai/Pune cold outreach in Phase 2.

## Punjab/Haryana State-Level Regulatory Note

- **Status:** Punjab (India) is the ONLY Indian state with active state-level agent regulation. Wave 1–3 already flagged this; Wave 4 search confirmed but could not surface the full 2025 statute text in clean form (results were polluted by Punjab-Pakistan Act 2025 and sub-municipal travel-agent license suspensions).
- **What we could confirm:** For Punjab India, operating an overseas-education + IELTS consultancy requires (a) **local municipality license**, (b) **incorporation certificate**, and (c) **GST registration** — sourced from edumilestones.com. The S.A.S. Nagar (Mohali) district portal hosts a "Foreign Consultants" information page, implying district-level registers exist. State news through 2026 references license cancellations/suspensions of travel-and-immigration agents, suggesting active enforcement.
- **Implications for selling SaaS to Punjab-based agencies:**
  - **Net positive for our SaaS** — registered agencies need a defensible records system (student consent forms, fee receipts, communication logs) for regulatory inspection. Pitch the product as "audit-ready" / "regulator-ready" record-keeping in Punjab. This is a wedge competitors don't talk about.
  - **DPDP Act 2023** layered on top → Punjab agencies face India-wide privacy law PLUS state-level conduct rules. A SaaS that ships DPDP-compliant consent capture and data-retention controls has a sharper pitch in Punjab than anywhere else.
  - **Risk:** the unregistered tail (~30–50% of Punjab shops) will be reluctant to put their full client list into a cloud SaaS. Mitigate with local-data-residency claim (Indian AWS region) and an export-anytime guarantee.
  - **Sales motion adjustment:** in Punjab, lead with "stay registration-ready" rather than "save counselor time." Founder needs one Punjab-licensed agency owner as early advisor.

## Market Entry Risks

**Launch metro (Delhi NCR + Chandigarh + Punjab):**
- AAERI-Delhi insiders may already be courting Meritto/SmartX; risk of incumbent lock-in on the marquee 5–8 agencies. Mitigate by approaching the AAERI-Delhi long tail (#10–#28 in size) first, not the top 3.
- Punjab state-level enforcement could change overnight (history of mid-year notifications); a regulatory shift is BOTH a risk (compliance feature work) AND an opportunity (forcing function for SaaS adoption).
- Founder-led in-person motion ceiling = ~20–25 customers before burnout; need 1 inside-sales hire by Month 4.
- "Uncle-network" trust dynamic means any single bad-faith counselor story spreads through AAERI WhatsApp in 24 hours — a single botched onboarding can poison 10 prospects.

**Expansion metros:**
- **Mumbai:** Meritto and Univariety have premium-segment lock-in on the top 5–6 W-India agencies. Entry path is the mid-tier (#10–#21).
- **Bangalore:** Installed-base risk is real — assume 40–50% of mid-size Bangalore agencies already pay for SOME SaaS (Leadsquared, Zoho, Meritto). Switching cost is the dominant barrier, not awareness.
- **Hyderabad:** G+A focus rate is the lowest of any Indian metro (US-MS dominates); only ~3–5 agencies are genuine ICP fits. Don't budget time here in Year 1.
- **Kerala/Chennai:** Gulf-medical and US-MS traditions mean G+A is a sideline; deprioritize through Year 1.

**Year-2 international:**
- DPDP cross-border data-transfer rules are still being notified; selling into Nepal/Bangladesh may need a data-residency story per jurisdiction by 2027.
- AU visa-rejection rates of 65% (Nepal), 51% (Bangladesh), 40% (India) for Feb 2026 are eroding AU-corridor counselor revenue, which compresses their software budgets. Germany-pivot story matters even more for Nepal/Bangladesh agencies than for Indian ones.

## Honest Bottom Line for the Founder

**Week 1: spend it in Connaught Place, Karol Bagh, and Sector 17 Chandigarh, not on a laptop.** The founder should pick 12 AAERI-listed agencies in Delhi NCR + Chandigarh, get 1 warm intro to each via the AAERI-Delhi WhatsApp circle (cheapest path: attend one AAERI quarterly event or sponsor a Goethe-Institut counselor mixer), and book 8–10 in-person discovery meetings in the first 14 days. Don't touch Mumbai, Bangalore, or Hyderabad until at least 10 of these 45–50 Delhi-Chandigarh-Punjab agencies are paying.

**Realistic India-only ceiling:** At ₹50k–₹2L ACV and ~120–150 deduplicated mid-size G+A agencies in India, the absolute ceiling is **₹1.2–3 Cr ARR** if you win 50% of the ICP — call it **₹1.5 Cr ARR sustainable, ₹3 Cr stretch.** That's a fundable seed business, not a Series A. So:

**Geo-expansion is not optional, it's structural.** Plan for Nepal entry by Month 13–15 (right after India Phase 4 wraps). Nepal alone could plausibly add ₹50–80L ARR — that's what moves the company from "seed-stage feature-rich tool" to "Series-A regional infrastructure." Don't start Nepal until the India motion runs without founder being in the meeting; but do start hiring the Kathmandu-or-Delhi-based BD person who'll own it by Month 10.
