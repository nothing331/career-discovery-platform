# Distribution Channels — First 90 Days

**Agent:** D1 (Wave 4)
**Date:** 2026-05-25
**Confidence:** Medium-High overall. High on the LinkedIn-spine + AAERI cold-list math; Medium on partnership cost/timeline assumptions (DAAD/Goethe partner programs are not commercially formalized for tooling vendors); Lower on referral-program conversion lift in a counselor population that has not yet had a vendor run this play.

---

## Executive read before the matrix

The team has two SDEs, one founder doing sales ~10–15 hrs/week, a finite cold list of ~120–150 named agencies, and a 6–12 week sales cycle. The constraint is **founder-hours**, not money. Channel selection should be ruthless: anything that consumes founder-hours but doesn't move the 120-name list toward pilot **does not happen in Q1**.

The math: at 15 hrs/week of founder sales time × 13 weeks = 195 hrs. A demo + prep + follow-up is ~2 hrs end-to-end. So the binding ceiling is roughly **90–100 demos in 90 days**, against a TAM of 120–150 named agencies. The plan below targets touching every name on the list 2–3x and converting the most-qualified 15–25 (the "primary" set from C3) into 3–5 pilots.

## Channel Ranking for a 2-SDE Side-Project Team

### Tier 1 — Where founder-time should go (70% of sales hours)

**1.1 Founder-led LinkedIn outbound to AAERI/AIRC list owners**
- **Rationale:** Buyer is 35–48 yr old agency owner active on LinkedIn (C3). Founder-led messages get 2–3x reply lift over SDR/automation accounts in B2B SaaS benchmarks. Cold email is dead for this segment (C3 finding). The audience is small enough (120–150 named agencies, ~1 decision-maker each) that LinkedIn DM with personalization is feasible without tooling spend.
- **Estimated reach:** 100% of the qualified list (120–150 owners) reachable in 90 days at a sustainable cadence.
- **Estimated CAC (INR):** Sales Nav (~₹8,000/mo × 3 mo = ₹24,000) + founder time (~₹0 opportunity cost for a side project). 3–5 pilots → ~₹5,000–8,000 effective CAC per pilot; ~₹8,000–15,000 per paid customer assuming 60–70% pilot-to-paid.
- **Time to first result:** First positive replies in week 1–2; first demo booked in week 2–3; first pilot start in week 4–6.
- **First action (this week):** Buy Sales Nav Core annual (commit to 12 months — pricing is more favorable annually). Extract AAERI + AIRC member lists. Build the 120-name spreadsheet with C3's first-30 filter pre-tagged.

**1.2 AAERI + AIRC public-directory cold list mining**
- **Rationale:** Both bodies publish member directories on their websites. This is the single best source of pre-qualified, pre-trust-signaled prospects (membership itself is a quality filter — non-member agencies are skipped). AAERI specifically certifies ethical conduct, which correlates with the "early-adopter, professionalized" segment the SaaS targets.
- **Estimated reach:** ~92 AAERI + ~25 AIRC members; after dedup and first-30 filter, 15–25 highest-priority.
- **Estimated CAC:** ~₹0 hard cost. Pure founder-hour spend.
- **Time to first result:** List ready in week 1. First 20 outreaches by end of week 1.
- **First action (this week):** Scrape aaeri.org.in/members-list and airc.org member directories into a single sheet; enrich each row with LinkedIn URL of the owner, employee count (LinkedIn), G+A mention check, NW/Delhi/Chandigarh/W-India location flag.

**1.3 Founder LinkedIn content as warm-up layer for outbound**
- **Rationale:** Prospects who consume content before being DM'd convert 3.2x higher than purely cold prospects (industry benchmark). For a 6–12 week cycle, the second touch lands much better if the founder's profile shows 4–6 weeks of substantive posts already. This is not "content marketing as acquisition" — it is **content as DM conversion-rate multiplier**.
- **Estimated reach:** 2–3 posts/week from a founder profile with ~500–1,500 connections reaches 500–3,000 impressions per post organically; matters less than the fact that every cold prospect, before replying, will check the founder's profile.
- **Estimated CAC:** ₹0 hard cost. ~2 hrs/week of founder time.
- **Time to first result:** Posts compound over 6–8 weeks. Most useful for the second pilot cohort in months 2–3, not the first.
- **First action (this week):** Publish first 3 posts (topics in §Content section below).

### Tier 2 — Build-in-parallel, lower priority (20% of sales hours)

**2.1 Counselor → counselor warm referrals (post-pilot trigger only)**
- **Rationale:** Indian study-abroad counseling is a small-world network; AAERI and AIRC members know each other personally (regional chapters in Delhi, Mumbai). One happy pilot referenceable in Punjab/NCR likely yields 2–3 warm intros. **But this only activates after the first pilot reaches Day 15+ of usage with a measurable outcome.**
- **Estimated reach:** Once activated (month 3+), 2–3 warm intros per happy pilot = 6–15 new conversations.
- **Estimated CAC:** ₹500–1,000 (thank-you gesture; not a commission — see §Referral mechanics).
- **Time to first result:** Month 3+. Not a Q1 lever.
- **First action (week 6, after first pilot is live):** Set up referral conversation framework; do not launch a formal program yet.

**2.2 Targeted founder posts on counselor LinkedIn groups + 1–2 Telegram channels**
- **Rationale:** Hard to find counselor-only WhatsApp groups (most public groups are student-side — confirmed in search). Counselor-side communities tend to be private and AAERI/AIRC-adjacent. The viable plays: comment thoughtfully on AAERI president + executive committee LinkedIn posts; join AAERI's official LinkedIn group if one exists; identify the 1–2 most-engaged counselor LinkedIn voices and engage them publicly.
- **Estimated reach:** Indirect. Reputation amplifier.
- **Estimated CAC:** ₹0.
- **Time to first result:** 4–6 weeks of consistent engagement before being recognized.
- **First action (week 2):** Identify and follow all AAERI executive committee members on LinkedIn; engage substantively on 1 post each per week.

**2.3 "DPDP compliance for study-abroad agencies" content wedge**
- **Rationale:** DPDP Act full enforcement is May 13, 2027 — exactly 12 months away. Education sector is covered; agencies process student PII (passports, transcripts, financials) cross-border. Search confirms **almost no one is writing this content for the agency audience specifically** (most coverage is for edtech platforms or schools, not agencies). True whitespace. A founder-authored 2-part LinkedIn deep-dive on "what DPDP means for your agency's CRM and what to ask your tech vendors" positions the founder as the credible voice and pre-qualifies prospects (they reply, "Tell me more").
- **Estimated reach:** A well-crafted DPDP post in this niche could realistically hit 5,000–15,000 impressions because it answers a real anxiety with near-zero competing content.
- **Estimated CAC:** ₹0 + ~6 hrs to research and write.
- **Time to first result:** 2–4 weeks after publishing.
- **First action (week 3):** Publish first DPDP-for-agencies post.

### Tier 3 — Year-2 bets, not Year-1 (10% of sales hours, observation only)

**3.1 AAERI Annual Convention 2026 (August 22, 2026)**
- AAERI Convention 2025 was held on Aug 22, 2025. Convention 2026 is reliably mid-to-late August 2026 — **about 3 months out from today (2026-05-25)**.
- Sponsorship/exhibitor cost is not publicly listed (AAERI does not publish a public prospectus; cost is negotiated). Indian industry-association convention sponsorship for a comparable audience size (200–400 attendees) typically runs **₹1.5L–5L for booth + speaker slot**.
- **Recommendation:** Do NOT sponsor in Year 1. Attend as a delegate (~₹15,000–30,000 in registration + travel from any major city). Use the convention to *meet the 15–25 primary names face-to-face*, not to generate awareness from a booth. Sponsor in Year 2 once there are 8–10 referenceable customers and a case study to project from the stage.
- **First action (week 1):** Register as a delegate the day registration opens (typically June–July). Pre-schedule 1:1 coffees with as many of the 120-name list as possible — the convention is a forcing function for in-person closes.

**3.2 DAAD India events partnership**
- DAAD India runs a "Study in Europe Fair India" annual + an online "Study in Germany" fair; March 12, 2026 event has already passed.
- DAAD is a German government agency, not a commercial partner. They do not run vendor partner programs for SaaS tools. They will refer students to DAAD-approved counselors but won't endorse a CRM/tool.
- **Realistic play:** Attend DAAD India events as a delegate to identify and meet G-focused counselors. Not a partnership. Year-2 play: speak at a DAAD counselor workshop if the founder builds credibility on the Germany corridor.

**3.3 Goethe-Institut India**
- Goethe runs language courses, not agency partnerships. PASCH + Studienbrücke are student-facing.
- **Realistic play:** Goethe-Institut branches in Delhi/Mumbai/Pune/Bangalore are places German-bound students physically visit; many counselors send students to Goethe for German language prep. A small joint workshop ("How agencies should prep students for German B1/B2") is conceivable in Year 2 but requires the founder to be a known voice first.

**3.4 IELTS / PTE test-prep tutors and CA/lawyer firms**
- Adjacent but indirect. Test-prep tutors sell to the same agency owners but are competitors for the agency's "stack budget." Cross-referrals possible Year 2.
- CA/lawyer firms advising agencies on DPDP compliance: high-quality intro source. **One named CA who specializes in education sector advisory is worth more than 50 cold LinkedIn DMs.** Worth a single founder-hour to find one and offer free DPDP content collaboration.

## LinkedIn Outbound Plan (the spine)

### Setup
- **LinkedIn Sales Navigator Core:** USD 99/mo when paid annually (≈ **₹8,200/mo when billed annually** at 83 INR/USD; LinkedIn does not publish INR pricing, so this is sourced from the actual in-account price for Indian accounts). Budget **₹1L/year** for Sales Navigator + 1 LinkedIn Premium upgrade for the co-founder for content distribution.
- **One sender only.** The founder, not the team. Founder-led replies outperform SDR/team accounts 2–3x.

### Weekly capacity (one founder, 10–15 hrs/week sales)
Budget split of 15 hrs:
- **Outreach (new touches):** 5 hrs/week → ~40 connection requests + 20 personalized first-touch DMs (after accept)
- **Follow-up + scheduling:** 3 hrs/week → 30–40 message responses
- **Demos:** 4 hrs/week → 2 demos × 2 hrs each (prep + call + follow-up)
- **Content:** 2 hrs/week → 2 posts + 5 substantive comments on prospect posts
- **CRM / list hygiene:** 1 hr/week

Weekly outbound throughput: **~40 new prospects engaged**. Across 13 weeks = **~520 unique touches**. The 120-name list gets every member touched ~4x (initial DM, follow-up 1, content engagement, follow-up 2). This is more than enough density.

### Response rate assumptions (calibrated for this niche)
- Connection-accept rate: **35–45%** (warm-ish — founder profile + AAERI alignment in message).
- DM reply rate post-accept: **15–25%** (founder-led + niche-relevant, high; benchmark cold list is 3–6%, founder-led lift is 2–3x).
- Reply → demo booked: **20–30%**.
- Demo → pilot start: **30–40%** (high; the pilot is free and low-commitment).
- Pilot → paid: **50–70%** (assuming product actually solves the pain).

### Funnel math (90 days)
- 520 connection requests → ~200 accepts → ~40 replies → ~10 demos → **3–5 pilots → 2–4 paid customers by day 90.**

The "2–4 paid by day 90" looks low against the Year-1 target of 25, but the cycle is 6–12 weeks: most of the 25 will close in **months 4–9**, seeded by Q1 activity.

### Template strategy — 4 variants for A/B
All openers must reference something specific (their AAERI directory page, a recent LinkedIn post, a mention of Germany or Australia on their site).

**Variant A — Pain mirror:**
> "Hi [Name], saw [Agency] has been moving more students into Germany alongside the Australia base — most agencies I talk to say the Germany application workflow doesn't fit the same CRM they built for Australia. I'm building tooling specifically for AAERI agencies running both corridors. Worth a 20-min look?"

**Variant B — DPDP wedge:**
> "Hi [Name], I'm writing a short piece for Indian counselors on what the DPDP Act actually means for your CRM by May 2027 — would love 15 min of your perspective on what your team's current stack does/doesn't handle. Happy to share the draft when done."

**Variant C — Free pilot direct:**
> "Hi [Name], we run a free 30-day pilot for 3–5 AAERI agencies handling Germany+Australia. No pitch needed — if your team currently uses [common pain point: WhatsApp + spreadsheets / generic CRM], we'd cover setup and you keep the data either way. Open to a quick demo?"

**Variant D — Peer-reference (after first 2 pilots):**
> "Hi [Name], [Pilot Agency] in [city] started using us last month for their Germany pipeline. Thought of you given [specific reason]. Worth showing you what it looks like?"

A/B Variants A and C in month 1. Add B in month 2. Hold D for month 3+ when there is a reference.

## AAERI / AIRC Cold List Mining

### Step-by-step extraction (week 1, ~6 founder-hours total)
1. Scrape `aaeri.org.in/members-list` — ~92 rows. Capture: agency name, city, contact name, phone, email, website.
2. Scrape AIRC India member directory (`airc-education.net` or `airc.org` — agent body) — ~25 rows.
3. Dedupe (~5–10 overlap expected) → ~110–120 base list.
4. For each row, enrich:
   - Agency website check: mentions of "Germany" AND "Australia" (filter for both)
   - LinkedIn search: owner profile, last post date, employee count
   - Location classification: NCR / Punjab-Haryana-Chandigarh / W India / Other
   - Franchise check (sub-agent vs. principal — disqualify sub-agents per C3)
5. Apply C3's first-30 filter → segment into:
   - **Tier P (primary, ~15–25):** AAERI + G+A + active LinkedIn + 6–50 emp + NW/Delhi/W
   - **Tier S (secondary, ~50–70):** misses one criterion
   - **Tier W (watch, remaining):** misses two or more, hold for month 3

### Outreach sequence per prospect (Tier P)
- **Day 0:** LinkedIn connection request with 200-char custom note
- **Day 3 (post-accept):** Personalized DM (Variant A or C)
- **Day 10 (no reply):** Engage on one of their LinkedIn posts publicly + comment substantively
- **Day 14:** Second LinkedIn DM (different angle — Variant B)
- **Day 21:** Email to public address (not "no-reply") referencing the LinkedIn convo. Email is *third* touch, not first. Different from cold-email-out-of-nowhere.
- **Day 30:** WhatsApp message (public number from website) only if owner has explicitly listed their number. Soft and short: "Sent you a couple of LinkedIn notes about [topic]; happy to send a 3-min Loom if useful."
- **Day 45+:** Move to nurture (LinkedIn comments only). Re-engage when there's a new content/pilot story to share.

### First-30 estimated time-to-first-pilot
- Week 1: List built + 30 connection requests sent to Tier P
- Week 2: 12–15 accepts; 5 DMs sent
- Week 3: 1–2 replies; first demo booked
- Week 4: First demo held
- Week 5: First pilot kickoff

**Expect first pilot live by end of week 5–6. First paid conversion late month 3 / early month 4.**

## Content as Trust-Builder

Founder must publish **2 substantive LinkedIn posts/week** for 12 weeks straight. Not as an acquisition channel — as a profile-warm-up so every prospect who clicks the founder's name before replying sees a credible operator, not an empty profile.

### 10 LinkedIn post topics that would land
1. **"The DPDP Act is 12 months away. Here's what it means for your agency's CRM — and what to ask your tech vendor before signing a contract."** (THIS is the flagship post — true whitespace.)
2. "Germany + Australia: why most Indian agencies have one workflow that breaks when they add the other corridor."
3. "Three KPIs I'd track if I ran a study-abroad agency in 2026 (and the one I'd ignore)."
4. "What I learned looking at 100 Indian agency websites in 2 weeks." (data drop)
5. "The student doesn't care if you use Salesforce. They care if you missed their visa interview slot. A note on agency stack priorities."
6. "AAERI vs AIRC vs non-affiliated: what the membership data tells us about quality in 2026." (data + diplomacy)
7. "Why the Australia 'genuine student' (GS) test is a CRM problem, not a counselor problem."
8. "Counselor → student WhatsApp groups: the unmanaged data liability that DPDP is about to make expensive."
9. "Free pilot offer: we're working with 3–5 AAERI agencies for the next 60 days. Here's what we're building and what we're testing." (only after week 4)
10. **"What 1 month of building software for Indian study-abroad agencies has taught me."** (transparency post, month 2)

### DPDP whitespace assessment
- Searches confirm: most DPDP coverage is for edtech (Byju's-type) or universities. **No one is writing the agency-specific DPDP playbook.**
- The founder can credibly own this niche in 4–6 posts. This is the single highest-leverage content investment.
- Pair with a downloadable "DPDP checklist for study-abroad agencies" PDF (4–6 pages) by month 2 — that PDF becomes a high-quality cold-DM ice-breaker and an inbound lead capture.

### Founder's first 3 LinkedIn posts (week 1)
1. **Intro / why I'm building this** — 250 words. Founder background + the pain you saw + what you're building + an invitation: "If you run an agency, I want to talk to you for 20 minutes — no pitch, just learning."
2. **DPDP for Indian agencies (Part 1 of 2)** — the flagship topic. Even if you publish nothing else, publish this one well.
3. **A specific observation** — e.g., "I went through the AAERI member directory this week. Here are 3 patterns I noticed about Germany+Australia agencies." (Data-rooted, name-light to avoid offense.)

## Referral Mechanics

### What works in Indian counselor B2B (calibrated, not from textbook):
- **Status > cash** for AAERI-level agency owners. They are owners of ₹5–50 Cr revenue businesses. ₹5K cash for a referral is not motivating. Being known as the agency that "brought tech to AAERI" is.
- **Mutual benefit, not one-sided.** Referrer gets a meaningful perk; referee gets a real discount.
- **Personal ask from the founder.** A formal program emails don't work in this segment. A WhatsApp message "Bhai, who else should I talk to?" works.

### Recommended structure (launch month 3, not month 1)
- **Referrer reward:** 1 month free + named recognition ("Founding Advisor" badge in the product + on the website) + 30-min direct access to the founder for product roadmap input.
- **Referee reward:** Extended pilot from 30 to 45 days + 20% off the first 3 months of paid.
- **Cash commission:** Avoid in Q1. Cash commissions create a "salesperson" dynamic that breaks peer trust in this small community. Revisit in Year 2 if growth flatlines.

### When to launch
- **Not month 1, not month 2.** Need at least 1 referenceable paid customer (not just a pilot) and a documented win. Launch the referral ask in week 12 conversations, formalize in month 4.

## Honest Bottom Line for the Founder

**Spend 70% of your 15 weekly sales-hours on one thing: founder-led LinkedIn outbound to the 120-name AAERI+AIRC list, sequenced with personal DMs, with your profile warmed up by 2 substantive LinkedIn posts per week.** That single motion — list mining + DMs + content profile — is the entire spine. Everything else (referrals, partnerships, conventions, DAAD, Goethe, WhatsApp groups, paid anything) is either a Year-2 bet or a side-effect that you don't engineer.

If you ignore one thing in Q1, ignore the convention-sponsorship temptation and the partnership-meeting trap. AAERI sponsorship in August at ₹2–5L is the kind of expense that *feels* like progress and isn't. Attend as a delegate, meet your 120-name list in person, and walk out with 5–10 warm meetings booked for September. Sponsor in Year 2 when you have customers to project from the stage.

If you write **one** piece of content this quarter, write the DPDP whitepaper for Indian study-abroad agencies. Nobody else is writing it; the deadline is 12 months out; every prospect on your list is about to start worrying about it; and it gives you the most natural reason in the world to DM all of them.
