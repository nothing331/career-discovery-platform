## Primary Persona: "Rajesh / Priya" — The Mid-Size Agency Owner

- **Name:** Rajesh Kumar (Delhi NCR) or Priya Shah (Ahmedabad/Pune) — pick whichever the founder finds easier to picture; the buying behavior is identical.
- **Age:** 35–50 (median ~42)
- **Role:** Founder / Managing Director / Head Counselor of a 5–50-person agency. In most cases all three titles belong to the same human. They still sit in on the "important" parent meetings (₹15L+ Australia masters cases, any "doctor's family" case from a small town).
- **Agency profile:**
  - 5–50 counselors total, of whom 2–6 are senior (5+ yrs experience, multi-destination), the rest junior (≤2 yrs, single-destination or visa-paperwork-only).
  - 1 main office in a Tier-1 metro (Delhi NCR / Mumbai / Bangalore / Hyderabad / Pune / Chandigarh / Ahmedabad) plus 0–3 satellite offices in nearby Tier-2 cities (e.g. Jalandhar, Ludhiana, Nagpur, Vadodara, Vijayawada, Coimbatore).
  - Multi-destination by necessity: Australia + UK + Canada + Germany + Ireland is the typical menu. **Pure Germany+Australia shops are rare** — only ~10–15% of the mid-size segment focus on these two as primary destinations. Most are weighted toward UK/Canada/Australia with Germany as a smaller-but-fastest-growing line.
  - Registered as Pvt Ltd or LLP; some run as proprietorship (especially solo founders who grew an agency around themselves).
  - Member of AAERI (if any Australia volume) and/or AIRC, ICEF, British Council. Memberships are displayed prominently in office and on website — they are the agency's trust signal.
- **Student volume per year:** 50–500 actual conversions (the upper 500–2,000 band is the top of this segment; 200 is the modal value). Lead volume is 5–10× the conversion volume.
- **Background (modal path):**
  - Started 8–20 years ago, often after working as a counselor at IDP / Y-Axis / Edwise / Canam / SIEC, or after running a coaching center (IELTS, GRE) that gradually pivoted to counseling.
  - Many founders studied abroad themselves (Australia, UK, US, or Germany) in the 1990s–2000s; their personal placement story is the agency's origin myth.
  - A meaningful minority are first-generation business founders from a non-counseling background (e.g. ex-banker, ex-visa-agent) who saw the commission economics and entered. These are more SaaS-curious than ex-counselors.
- **Goals (ranked by what they say vs. what they actually buy):**
  1. **More paid students per month** — every other goal is downstream of this. Top of funnel → enquiry → counseling session → application → paid case.
  2. **Higher fee per student** — pushing students into "premium" packages (₹50K–₹2L upfront fees) instead of the commodity "₹10K + commission" path.
  3. **Reduce head-counselor time per student** — they correctly intuit they are the bottleneck. The 2–3 hr discovery session is recognized as a problem.
  4. **Junior counselors who can run a session unsupervised** — the holy grail. Most have failed to train junior staff to head-counselor quality and have stopped trying.
  5. **Defend against bigger agencies and against ChatGPT-armed parents** — explicit pain in 2025–26 conversations.
  6. **Build a brand that survives the founder** — a real long-term aspiration but rarely the trigger for a near-term purchase.
- **Frustrations (in their own voice):**
  - "Every parent now walks in with a ChatGPT printout. I spend the first 30 minutes correcting it."
  - "I can't take a vacation. If I'm not in the room for the big case, the family walks."
  - "My junior counselor knows Australia. She does not know Germany. I have to do every Germany case myself."
  - "Australia visa rules changed three times in 2024–25. My team is still on the old playbook."
  - "Leverage / Yocket are eating our top-of-funnel with their YouTube. We can't compete on content."
  - "We already pay for Meritto / SmartX / a custom Excel-CRM. I do NOT need another tool."
  - "I gave one student a beautifully formatted university shortlist last month. The family WhatsApp'd it to a cousin's agency in Hyderabad and got the visa filed there for ₹5K less. I will not put my IP in someone else's PDF tool."
- **Current tools (typical stack):**
  - **CRM:** Meritto (Premium Edition for study-abroad agencies — likely ₹15–50K/mo) **or** SmartX CRM (₹4K/mo solo, more for teams) **or** a custom Zoho CRM **or** Excel + Google Sheets. Roughly 40% on a real CRM, 60% still on Excel/Sheets/WhatsApp [Estimate, Medium-Low confidence].
  - **University shortlisting:** ApplyBoard / Edvoy / Studyportals / Hotcourses (free agent-facing tools), supplemented by the head counselor's memory and a curated Excel sheet of "schools I trust."
  - **Communications:** WhatsApp Business (universal), Gmail/Outlook, occasional Zoom or Google Meet for international university calls.
  - **Document prep:** MS Word + PDF, Canva for marketing one-pagers, ChatGPT (Free or ₹1,650/mo Plus) for SOP drafting and Q&A.
  - **Marketing:** Instagram (universal), Facebook page (declining), website on WordPress, LinkedIn Personal account active, paid Google/Meta ads for the larger end of the segment only.
  - **Accounting/back-office:** Tally or Zoho Books; junior staff handles.
- **Software budget per year (per Wave 1 A1):** ₹60K–₹6L total tooling spend; mid-size weighted average ~₹1.5L. **Realistic addressable budget for a NEW point-solution like ours: ₹36K–₹1.8L per year (~₹3K–₹15K/month), midpoint ~₹80K/yr.** This is the ceiling before they start asking "why not just expand our Meritto contract?"
- **Time-on-tool tolerance:** **Demo length: 20–30 minutes maximum.** Anything longer is read as "this team doesn't respect my time." Evaluation window: **2–4 weeks** from first demo to pilot kickoff, **4–8 weeks** from pilot kickoff to paid decision. They will absolutely use the full 30-day pilot but the actual decision is made in the first 10 days based on whether they personally generated a PDF and showed it to one real student family.
- **Tech adoption profile:** Pragmatist, not enthusiast. They are on WhatsApp 12+ hours a day; they use ChatGPT casually but distrust it; they have NEVER read a SaaS pricing page from top to bottom. **Will not sign up for a free trial that asks for a credit card. Will sign up for a free trial that asks only for a phone number + WhatsApp consent.**
- **Quote (synthesized from Wave 2 + founder anecdote — flag as inferred, not direct):**
  > "Look, the idea sounds good. But I won't pay for it until I see one of my counselors actually using it on a Monday morning with a real parent in the room. Send me the link, I'll try it this weekend. If my junior makes a PDF in 10 minutes that I would have spent two hours on — call me Tuesday." *(Inferred persona voice, not a direct quote from a named customer.)*

## Buying Behavior (Primary Persona)

- **How they discover tools:**
  1. **LinkedIn — own network feed** (primary). Indian counselors are heavy LinkedIn users — they post placements, certifications (AAERI/AIRC badges visible on profiles per Wave 2), university partnership announcements. A founder-led LinkedIn presence is the single highest-ROI discovery channel.
  2. **WhatsApp counselor-only groups** (high-trust but gated). 3–10 active groups across the country, organized by region (AAERI Delhi-NCR, AAERI Chandigarh, AAERI West India, etc.) and by topic (visa updates, Germany specialists, Australia specialists). Membership requires warm intro. Pitches inside the group are unwelcome; sharing useful artifacts (visa rule cheat-sheet, Germany Studienkolleg eligibility table) is welcome.
  3. **AAERI / AIRC / ICEF India events and webinars.** AAERI Annual Convention (Aug, per Facebook post) and ICEF India workshops are concentrated buyer pools. Sponsor a session for ₹50K–₹2L, walk away with 20–40 warm intros (per Wave 2 GTM).
  4. **Word of mouth from a peer agency owner** — the conversion driver after discovery. "Mukesh from Lucknow swears by this thing" closes deals that 5 demos cannot.
  5. **Cold outbound LinkedIn DMs** — mixed reception but the highest-volume founder-led channel; converts at 2–4% to pilot if personalized [Wave 2 estimate].
  6. **Google search** — almost never. They do not search "best CRM for study abroad agency." If they do, they get Meritto / SmartX SEO; we will not outrank.
  7. **Inbound from PLG free-PDF generator** — net new in 2026; could be 10–20% of pipeline if executed (Wave 2).
- **Who's involved in the decision:**
  - **Solo / 1–4 counselor shop:** owner = buyer = primary user. One-person decision.
  - **5–15 counselor shop (the modal Wave 3 target):** owner makes the call after one informal check with their head ops person and one "would you actually use this" question to a senior counselor. **2 informal yes-votes = green light.** No procurement process, no security review.
  - **20–50 counselor shop (upper end of segment):** owner + operations head + 1 senior-counselor reviewer. Slightly more formal; an Excel comparison sheet may appear. Procurement is still founder-driven and informal compared to Western enterprise norms.
- **Decision criteria (ranked by what actually moves the deal):**
  1. **"Will my junior counselor actually use this on Monday?"** — usability over feature breadth. If it requires training, they won't deploy it.
  2. **"Does the PDF make me look good to the parent?"** — disintermediation question. The "counselor's package" section is decisive (per project memo). They will inspect the output PDF byte-by-byte before signing.
  3. **"What does it cost me, predictably, per month?"** — flat-rate or tiered-by-seat. Usage-based pricing is mistrusted ("what if it spikes?"). Per Hindustan Metro 2025: flexible pricing erodes trust if can't be explained in 5 minutes.
  4. **"Will the founder pick up my call when something breaks?"** — they want a WhatsApp number for the founder, not a support ticket portal. Indian SMBs buy from people, not platforms (Martal.ca SMB Sales 2025).
  5. **"Is the data safe? Will it leak to competitors?"** — data privacy concern is real but unsophisticated. They want a verbal assurance ("we don't share data") more than a SOC 2 report. DPDP Act compliance is a 2027 concern, not 2026.
  6. **"Does it integrate with Meritto / my current CRM?"** — soft preference; not a blocker if the tool stands alone and is easy to use.
  7. **Brand / trust signals:** AAERI / AIRC association, testimonials from named agencies, founder LinkedIn profile, "100+ agencies use us" claim. ISO/SOC 2 not required at this segment.
- **Typical software evaluation window:** **2–4 weeks demo-to-pilot; 4–8 weeks pilot-to-paid.** Total: 6–12 weeks from first touch to first invoice. Faster (3–4 weeks) if the founder is already in pain (e.g. lost a junior counselor, just had a bad parent meeting); slower (12–16 weeks) if it's a "good to have" framing.
- **Sales cycle:** **Distinctly faster than Western SMB norms** (which are 3–6 months) because there is no committee, no formal RFP, no security review. **Distinctly slower than self-serve B2B SaaS** (which is days) because they will not sign up without a human conversation. Plan for **6–10 weeks median, founder-led** (consistent with Meritto's "100 demos in 100 days" cadence, Wave 2).
- **Payment terms preferred:** **Annual upfront with 10–15% discount, or quarterly upfront.** Monthly auto-debit is read as "they expect to churn me." UPI / NEFT / cheque preferred over credit card. Net-30 invoicing is acceptable for 20+ counselor shops; not for smaller. (Wave 2 GTM.)
- **Common objections (and likely response framing — for context, not as deliverable):**
  - **"ChatGPT does this for free."** → "ChatGPT gives the parent a generic answer. Our PDF is branded as YOUR agency, includes the visa-fee math for Germany Aubildungs-pathway, and ends with a counselor's-package page that makes the family commit to you, not to a chatbot."
  - **"Our team has the knowledge internally."** → "Your head counselor does. Your junior on the third floor doesn't. This tool puts your head counselor's playbook into a 10-minute artifact your junior can run."
  - **"Our students will hate getting an AI-generated PDF."** → True risk. Counter: the PDF is co-branded with the agency, signed off by the counselor, framed as a "personalized counselor's package" — not as an AI report.
  - **"Data privacy — we can't put student info in your tool."** → Honest answer: encrypted-at-rest, India data residency, no LLM training on customer data, DPDP-aligned. Get the verbal assurance into the pilot agreement.
  - **"Why do I need this when we have Meritto/SmartX already?"** → Meritto is a CRM (lead → application pipeline). We are a discovery tool (parent meeting → PDF artifact). Different job. We integrate, we don't replace.
  - **"I'll think about it / send me a deck."** → Real reason: not enough pain yet, or they're polite. Re-engage in 6 weeks during the next intake cycle.
  - **"What's the catch / why is it cheap?"** → Indian SMBs are suspicious of low prices. Need to frame as "founding-customer pricing for first 30 agencies; goes up after."

## Anti-Persona — Who is NOT the customer

- **B2C-only platforms (Yocket, Leap Scholar, Edvoy, Shiksha).** They are competitors for the student (Wave 2), not buyers of our SaaS. **Do not pitch them.** Possible partner play in year 2, not a buyer.
- **Universities directly (German DAAD-listed schools, Australian Group of Eight).** That's Cialfo / Univalley / ApplyBoard territory. We are not the right wedge — they buy enrollment-management tools, not counselor-enablement.
- **Government counseling cells (NCERT, state board career cells, Atal Tinkering Labs).** Long sales cycle (12–24 months), low budget (₹10K–₹50K per district), wrong fit. The pre-pivot direction we explicitly left in 2026-05-13.
- **US/UK/Canada-only specialists.** Destination mismatch at launch — we are Germany+Australia beachhead. Year 2 expansion possibly.
- **K-12 schools and college career-counseling departments.** Cialfo's turf. Different buyer, different motion, different price point.
- **Coaching centers (IELTS, GRE, GMAT prep).** Adjacent but wrong buyer. They monetize test prep, not admission counseling.
- **Sub-agents (counselors who sub-contract under a bigger agency).** Real segment but they don't have buying authority — the parent agency does.
- **Tier-3 city solo consultants.** Below the floor of our pricing willingness-to-pay; ARPU too low to support founder-led sales.

## Buying Triggers — when does Rajesh actually pick up the phone?

1. **Intake-season prep panic** — Apr–Aug for Sep intake (Germany + Australia); Oct–Dec for Feb intake (Australia). Founders are most receptive to "save your head counselor 50 hours" pitches in **April–June and October–November.** Avoid Jul–Aug (peak workload, won't take demos) and Dec–Jan (winter slowdown, low urgency).
2. **A junior counselor just quit / a senior just got poached.** Knowledge-loss panic = highest-urgency buying moment. Hard to time but high signal in LinkedIn "we're hiring" posts.
3. **A bad parent meeting with a ChatGPT-armed family** — the "I lost a case to a chatbot" moment is now a real driver in 2026 conversations.
4. **A visa-rule change that breaks the playbook** — Australia (post-2024 cap on subclass 500, fall 2025 capacity rules), Germany (Aubildungs-pathway expansions 2025), UK graduate-route changes. Counselors scramble for updated frameworks; we can be the framework.
5. **DPDP Act compliance deadline (May 2027)** — by Q4 2026 mid-size agencies will be asking vendors about data protection. A vendor with India-residency + DPDP-DPA-ready is differentiated. Not a Q2 2026 driver yet.
6. **Competitive loss to Leverage/Yocket** — when an agency loses 3+ students in a quarter to a B2C player, they look for a defensive tool. The "counselor's package" framing speaks directly to this.
7. **Peer agency owner ROI testimonial** — once 5 paid customers exist, every "Mukesh's agency closed 4 more cases this month using this thing" message in a WhatsApp group is a buying trigger for the next 10.

## Honest Bottom Line for the Founder

**The realistic first paying customer is a 35–48-year-old founder of a 6–20-counselor mid-size agency in Delhi NCR, Chandigarh, Pune, Ahmedabad, or Bangalore; AAERI member; offers both Germany and Australia (with Australia as the larger volume line); active on LinkedIn personally; converts 100–300 students a year; runs the agency on Meritto-or-Excel + WhatsApp + ChatGPT-Plus; spends ₹60K–₹2L per year on tooling; will buy at ₹3K–₹15K/month after a 30-day free pilot in which their own junior counselor produced a PDF that visibly impressed a real parent.**

**The single most important channel to reach them is LinkedIn — personalized founder DMs to AAERI/AIRC member-agency owners (combined ~120–150 named targets after dedupe), backed by founder LinkedIn content that compounds over 9–12 months, and one AAERI Annual Convention appearance in August.** Everything else (events, WhatsApp groups, PLG free-PDF, referrals) is supporting; LinkedIn is the spine.

## Data Gaps

- **No direct ethnographic data on Indian study-abroad counselor day-in-life.** The 2–3hr discovery-session claim comes from the founder's own informal customer conversations (project memo), not from a published time-study. **Critical to validate in first 5 design-partner interviews** — if discovery sessions are actually 60–90 minutes, the time-saving pitch loses 40% of its punch.
- **No public list of WhatsApp counselor groups.** Their existence is asserted in Wave 2; their density, moderation rules, and openness to a new SaaS vendor are anecdotal. Need a warm intro to map.
- **No public data on Indian mid-size agency software-budget actuals** — Wave 1 estimates triangulated from CRM vendor pricing pages. Validate budget tolerance in first 5 design partner conversations explicitly.
- **No confirmed count of "agencies that focus on Germany+Australia specifically"** — estimated at 10–15% of mid-size segment (200–375 agencies), but unverified. AAERI is Australia-by-definition; no equivalent India body for Germany. **DAAD India does not maintain an agency directory.** This is a real gap.
- **No data on counselor turnover within mid-size agencies** — the "knowledge walks out the door" pain is asserted but not quantified. A real number ("60% of junior counselors leave within 18 months") would sharpen the pitch.
- **No confirmation of which named agencies are doing Australia + Germany specifically** beyond hand-confirmed ones (SIEC, Valmiki Group, AEC Overseas, Competitive Careers). A founder-led mapping pass over the 92 AAERI agencies' websites would close this gap in 4–6 hours of work.
- **No data on conversion rates from cold LinkedIn DM → demo → pilot for THIS specific niche.** Wave 2 estimates of 2–4% pilot conversion are extrapolated from Indian B2B SaaS norms, not measured for study-abroad-agency-as-buyer. **Will only be known after the first 100 DMs go out.**
- **Tertiary persona enterprise procurement requirements** (specifically SOC 2 / DPDP DPA expectations from Y-Axis / IDP-scale buyers) are unmapped because we are not pursuing them year 1. Re-research in Q4 2026 before year-2 white-label motion.
